AIAA D/B/F, 2017

Team Vayuputras

The AIAA Foundation Design/Build/Fly (DBF) competition is a partnership between the AIAA Foundation, Cessna Aircraft Company, and Raytheon Missile Systems. The contest provides a real-world aircraft design experience for engineering students by giving them the opportunity to validate their analytic studies.

  • Tucson, USA
  • The team this year consisted of seven members who designed “Tube-Launched UAV”
  • Team Vayuputras finished with the top design report score out of 95 teams and secured the 7th rank overall.

AIAA D/B/F, 2016

Team Vayuputras
  • Wichita, Kansas, USA.
  • The objective for this year was to build two aircrafts designed such that one occupies other completely.
  • Team Vayuputras consisting of 8 members made a record of standing 2th in the design report contest and 39th overall among 90 teams across the globe.

Future Flight Design, 2015

Team Vayuputras

Future Flight Design (FFD) is an international contest on designing “Aerial Robotic Vehicles for the Future" organized by the Turkish Air Force Academy. FFD provides a platform to bring about different and original UAV designs.

  • Istanbul, Turkey.
  • Team Vayuputras designed an unmanned mothership aircraft deploying a swarm of micro air vehicles (MAV) over the target area.
  • The team stood third internationally, first in India.

AIAA D/B/F, 2014

Team Phoenix
  • Wichita, Kansas, USA.
  • Team Phoenix was the first team from AeroVJTI to participate in AIAA D/B/F competition. The team consisted of 8 members.
  • Plane UDAAN having 3m wingspan was split up into 3 parts considering the transportation and assembly. It was powered using twin electric propulsion system to carry an empty weight of 2.2kg.
  • Team Phoenix stood 32nd for the design report with a score of 85.5/100. It was ranked 1st from India, 3rd from Asia, and 48th overall from 100 teams participating internationally.

SAE AERO DESIGN WEST (REGULAR CLASS), 2012

The Golden Eagles
  • Los Angeles, California, USA
  • A team of 8 members designed Bramhastra with a wing span of 3m, 4.5kg self-weight, 20kg payload carrying capacity and an IC Engine driven propulsion system.
  • The team maintained their glory by securing the 21st rank internationally. The mighty BRAMHASTRA still remains the largest plane built by our club.

SAE AERO DESIGN WEST (ADVANCED CLASS), 2012

Team Vihang
  • Van Nuys, California, USA
  • A team of 8 members designed Plane Vihang with a twin tail configuration, winglets and IC Engine driven propulsion system.
  • The team won 1st prize in oral presentation and stood 6th overall internationally.

SAE AERO Design, 2011

SAE Aero design includes two regional competitions that challenge participating teams to conceive, design, fabricate, and test a radio-controlled aircraft that can take off, land and optimally meet the mission requirements.

  • Fort Worth, Texas, USA
  • A team of 6 members designed Plane Viyaccra with highly efficient pusher prop configuration and an optimized structure and twin boom tail for effective control.
  • 5thinternationally in Design Report and 21 st Overall score on oral presentation, design report and flight.
